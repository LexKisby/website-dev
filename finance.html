Financial Technology

Overview:

Financial technology is defined as 'a new financial industry that applies technology to improve financial activities.'

History:

  1. 1867 - Edward A. Calahan invents printing telegraph for stock prices.
  2. 1884 - Herman Hollerith files a patent for the Tabulator. This was a device that could rapidly read numbers encoded by punching holes onto a set of cards. Its basic function was to count or add numbers represented by the holes and print them on paper.
  3. 1885 - William Burrough files a patent for a mechanical adding machine. It printed numbers, added them up, then printed the sum.
  4. 1948 - IBM introduced the 604 Electronic Calculating Punch. This could perform addition, subtraction, multiplication and division very quickly.
  5. 1971 - Donald C. Wetzel receives a patent for the 'automatic teller banking machine. This used a magnetically coded strip on a plastic card along with a personal identification number which allowed users to withdraw cash from their bank account.
  6. 1977 - Don Coppersmith, a mathematician and cryptographer, develops the Data Encryption Standard.
